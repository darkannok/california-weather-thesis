## Climate Background (Mediterranean climate)

A Mediterranean climate (/ˌmɛdɪtəˈreɪniən/ MED-i-tə-RAY-nee-ən), also called a dry summer climate, Mediterranean summer-wet climate, or simply Mediterranean climate, is a temperate climate type characterized by dry summers and mild, wet winters. This climate type is found in the "Mediterranean" regions of Europe, California, and parts of Western and South Australia. It is classified under the Köppen system as Csa or Csb, depending on whether the dry season is very hot (Csa) or mild (Csb).

Key characteristics:
- Hot, dry summers (typically June–September)
- Mild, rainy winters (typically December–March)
- Annual precipitation between 300–1000 mm, mostly in winter
- Vegetation: sclerophyllous shrublands, olive trees, vineyards

Regions: California (especially coastal areas like Los Angeles), central Chile, southwestern Australia, Cape Town region of South Africa, and the Mediterranean Basin.
